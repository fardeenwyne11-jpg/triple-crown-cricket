Whitewash — the free cricket squad draft game

What is Whitewash?

Whitewash is a free T20 franchise cricket draft game where you build an all-time XI from nearly 800 player-seasons across the IPL, PSL, Big Bash, CPL and Vitality Blast (2008–today).

Spin the wheel to land on a real franchise squad, draft one of its players, and build your XI one position at a time — mixing modern match-winners with legends from every era.

Then take your team through four tournaments — the IPL, PSL, Big Bash and Champions League. Win all four for the Grand Slam, sweep the Champions League unbeaten for the Whitewash, and find out just how strong your dream XI really is.

How to play

1
Spin the wheel
Each spin lands on a real franchise squad from a specific season.
2
Draft a player
Pick one player from that squad and slot them into your XI.
3
Build your XI
Repeat until all 11 positions are filled with franchise talent.
4
Win the trophies
Take your XI through four tournaments and chase the Grand Slam — then the Whitewash.

Frequently asked questions

Is Whitewash free to play?
Yes — Whitewash is completely free and runs right in your browser. There's nothing to buy.
What is the Grand Slam and the Whitewash?
The Grand Slam means winning all four trophies — the IPL, PSL, Big Bash and Champions League — in a single run. The Whitewash is the ultimate: a Grand Slam where you also sweep the Champions League without losing a single game.
Which tournaments can I win?
Four T20 tournaments in a locked difficulty progression: the Big Bash, then the PSL, the IPL, and finally the Champions League — the toughest of the lot.
How many players and squads are in the game?
Nearly 800 player-seasons across 71 franchise squads, drawn from the IPL, PSL, Big Bash, CPL and Vitality Blast and spanning 2008 to the present.
Can I play on mobile?
Yes. Whitewash is built mobile-first and works on phones, tablets and desktop.
Is Whitewash affiliated with the IPL, ICC or any league?
No. Whitewash is an independent project and is not affiliated with, endorsed by or sponsored by the ICC, any league, franchise or player. All names are used for identification purposes only.
